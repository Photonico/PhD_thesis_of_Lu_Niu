\textbf{Optical properties}

The optical observables used in this chapter are obtained from the frequency-dependent dielectric function.
Their definitions,
including the absorption coefficient $\alpha_{\lambda}(\omega)$,
loss function $L_{\lambda}(\omega)$,
refractive index $n_{\lambda}(\omega)$,
extinction coefficient $k_{\lambda}(\omega)$,
and reflectivity $R_{\lambda}(\omega)$,
are introduced in Section~\ref{sec:from_dielectric_function_to_optical_properties}.
Therefore,
the duplicate formula definitions are not repeated here.

\textbf{Renormalization of the dielectric function to account for the vacuum region}

The thickness of the 2D systems,
$d_{\mathrm{2D}}$,
is required for the dielectric-function renormalization introduced in Section~\ref{2_calculation_methods}.
It is defined by taking the difference in the $z$ coordinate of the top and bottom atoms in the system,
$dz$,
and adding an additional van der Waals distance on each side of the monolayer and bilayer systems.
For the monolayer and bilayer,
the top and bottom atoms are the pyramidal apical B atoms.
For the H-terminated bilayer,
the top and bottom atoms are the H atoms.
Here,
we use $d_{\mathrm{vdW}}=\qty{1.91}{\angstrom}$ for boron and $d_{\mathrm{vdW}}=\qty{1.2}{\angstrom}$ for hydrogen.
A schematic illustration of the total thickness,
$D_{\mathrm{total}}\equiv d_{\mathrm{2D}}$,
is shown in figure~\ref{fig:S2.6}.
