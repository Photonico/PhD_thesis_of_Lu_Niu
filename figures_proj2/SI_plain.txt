{\bf Optical properties}

Using the dielectric function, the absorption coefficient, $\alpha(\omega)$, 
is obtained below
where $c$ is the speed of light.
\begin{equation}
    \alpha(\omega) = \frac{\sqrt{2}\omega}{c} \left[ \left( \epsilon_{1}^{2}(\omega)+\epsilon_{2}^{2}(\omega) \right)^{\frac{1}{2}} - \epsilon_{1}(\omega)  \right]^{\frac{1}{2}}
\label{eqn:adsorption_coefficient}
\end{equation}
The loss function, $L(\omega)$, is obtained using the expression:
\begin{equation}
    L(\omega) = \frac{\epsilon_{2}(\omega)}{\epsilon_{1}^{2}(\omega)+\epsilon_{2}^{2}(\omega)}
    \label{eqn:loss_function}
\end{equation}
The refractive index $n(\omega)$, extinction coefficient $k(\omega)$ and 
reflectivity $R(\omega)$ are calculated using Eq.~(\ref{eqn:Refractive_index}),  
(\ref{eqn:extinction_coefficient}) and  (\ref{eqn:Reflectivity}) 
respectively. In Eq.~(\ref{eqn:Reflectivity}), 
$k$ is the extinction coefficient and
$n$ is the refractive index.
\begin{equation}
    n(\omega) = \left[ \frac{\sqrt{\epsilon_{1}^{2}+\epsilon_{2}^{2}}+\epsilon_{1}}{2} \right]^{\frac{1}{2}}
    \label{eqn:Refractive_index}
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
    k(\omega) = \left[ \frac{\sqrt{\epsilon_{1}^{2}+\epsilon_{2}^{2}}-\epsilon_{1}}{2} \right]^{\frac{1}{2}}
    \label{eqn:extinction_coefficient}
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
R(\omega) = \frac{(n-1)^{2}+k^{2}}{(n+1)^{2}+k^{2}}
    \label{eqn:Reflectivity}
\end{equation}

{\bf Renormalization of the dielectric function to account for the vacuum region}
The thickness of the 2D systems as required in Eqs. 6 and 7 of the manuscript
is defined by taking the 
difference in the $z$-coordinate of the top and bottom atoms in the 
system ($dz$), i.e. the pyramidal (apical) B atoms for monolayer and bilayer, 
and H atoms for H-terminated bilayer, plus
an additional dvdW distance on either side of the monolayer and 
bilayer systems to account for the van der Waals radius of the outer atoms. 
In this case we use dvdW = 1.91 \AA, for Boron and dvdW = 1.2 \AA, 
for Hydrogen. A schematic illustration how the total thickness 
(Dtotal $\equiv d_{\rm 2D}$) is calculated is shown in Fig.~S6.
